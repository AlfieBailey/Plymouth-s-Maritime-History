HMNB Devonport

Operated by the Royal Navy, HMNB Devonport is 1 of 3 Naval Bases in use in the UK to this day, and the only nuclear repair and refuelling facility for the Royal Navy. The base began as a Royal Navy Dockyard in the late 17th century, designed and built on open ground by Edmund Dummer as an integrated facility for the repair and maintenance of warships. Over the 2 following centuries, it expanded, and in the 20th century reached its current extent. The yard was known as HM Dockyard, Plymouth until 1843, when it was renamed HM Dockyard, Devonport. Then, in the late 20th century,'Naval Base' replaced 'Dockyard' in the official naval designation. The Naval Base as a whole covers an area of 650 acres (2.6 km2) with 4 miles (6 km) of waterfront; it has 25 tidal berths, five basins and fourteen dry docks (docks numbered 1 to 15, but there is no 13 Dock). The base employs 2,500 service personnel and civilians and contributes to approximately 10% of Plymouth's income
